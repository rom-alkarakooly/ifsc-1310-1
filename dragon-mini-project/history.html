Our History

SpaceX, founded in 2002 by entrepreneur Elon Musk, was established with the ambitious goal of reducing space transportation costs and making space exploration viable for future human settlement on other planets. SpaceX's journey has been marked by a series of historic achievements that have redefined spaceflight.

SpaceX and Dragon Milestones

  • 2002: SpaceX is founded in Hawthorne, California, by Elon Musk.
  • 2004: Development of the Dragon spacecraft begins, intended to transport cargo to the International Space Station (ISS).
  • 2008: SpaceX’s Falcon 1 rocket becomes the first privately developed liquid-fueled rocket to reach Earth’s orbit, laying the groundwork for future Dragon missions.
  • 2010: Dragon makes history as the first commercial spacecraft to return safely to Earth from orbit.
  • 2012: Dragon becomes the first commercial spacecraft to deliver cargo to the ISS and safely return, marking a new era in private spaceflight.
  • 2014: NASA selects Dragon for its Commercial Crew Program, with the goal of transporting astronauts to the ISS.
  • 2020: Dragon successfully launches the first crewed mission, the Crew Dragon Demo-2, marking the return of human spaceflight capabilities to the United States.

Dragon Development Timeline

The Dragon spacecraft was conceived as a versatile, reusable vehicle capable of carrying both cargo and crew to low Earth orbit and beyond. It has undergone several key phases of development and testing to reach its current capabilities:

  • Phase 1 - Cargo: Dragon’s initial design focused on cargo missions under NASA’s Commercial Orbital Transportation Services (COTS) program, resulting in successful ISS resupply missions.
  • Phase 2 - Crew Dragon: In collaboration with NASA’s Commercial Crew Program, Dragon was adapted to transport astronauts, featuring advanced safety systems like the emergency launch escape system and environmental controls for crew comfort.
  • Phase 3 - Dragon Lab: Designed for standalone missions, Dragon Lab provides a microgravity research platform for a variety of scientific and technological experiments.
